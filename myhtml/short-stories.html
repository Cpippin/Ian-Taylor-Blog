Beauregaurd:
The Beast

I saved my family from a beast once. It's true, I really did. Like any other day, I awoke imagining it would be quiet, uneventful and just plain boring. Now that's not to say that seeing my family in danger is exciting for me, or that I dislike the quiet, much to the contrary. Nonetheless, when I need to protect those I love, I am relentless, and this situation was no different.

My brain could not decipher what exactly was going on. I knew that the noise was loud and seemed threatening, and there were children near by, but I had no clue what this thing was capable of, so I had to take action. I gathered a deep breath into my lungs and prepared to expel what was building in my vocal cords. With every yell, every cry, every bark of ferocity, the monster retreated. I had scared it, I wasn't sure why, or how, but I knew I had to continue. Again I lurched forward, exercising every fiber of my body to scare it back, scare it away from the helpless kids.

I was preparing to finish my barrage when the creature redoubled it's efforts and lunged back at me. I got a good look at the beast now. It was tall and slender with a hard dark red exterior. The bottom of it, where I assume the mouth must've been, was wider than the rest of the body. In a way resembling a hammerhead shark, with teeth just as ferocious. It made a loud noise that didn't ever stop for the duration of the battle. This pattern continued, it growling and lurching towards me, I yelling and jumping towards it. I could not muster the courage to actually make contact with it, and it seemed that this thing had the same apprehension, regardless, I still flinched every time it moved in my direction.

This went on for some time. A test of wills. As though I were the gods, and it the titans of ancient Greco mythology. There was no progression made for a long period of time, I began to grow weary. I retreated to the position of most importance, the children, for my final stand. When I got there, I was met with gratitude and reverence for my efforts, but the battle was not over.

As the creature drew nearer, I began to panic. What would happen if I could not protect this family? I decided not to think of the possibility. Instead I turned my fear into adrenaline and made my final assault on the tormentor. Finally, with my last bark, I drove the vacuum away.

Trapped:
5 Second Prison

Live mannequins
Every few minutes, change position, and hold again.
Brightly colored crepe streamers running down around them.
Holding drinks they never sip from.
Trying to blink as little as possible.

A man walks up to gaze.
Skin caked with dirt from some unknown place and fingernails full of everything he has held dear.
Pushing in passed the crowd and up to the glass, his eyes reveal years. Mysterious years that are half forgotten, bearing a heavy weight on his mind.
Why is he here? Maybe he doesn't even know. Out of place, or the most real representation of the human experience on the street?
He reaches his hand down, clutching the bottom of his shirt, and reveals his hairy abdomen to the unsuspecting pieces of art.

Surprised onlookers, gazing at the unabashed display, freeze waiting to see what will happen next.
None focused on the other man.

None but me.

Taking bold strides, he is a man of authority, or so he thinks. Pollo shirt tucked into his leather belted pants and black shoes to match, he wears a badge too insignificant to be memorable. His face solemn and solid like an unmoving sculpture in the side of a lonely mountain. His steps in perfect timing, as though he is listening to his favorite track and planning his movement accordingly.

By the time he makes it over to the would be stripper, everyone has noticed him. But eyes are on the dirty provocateur once again almost immediately.

His other hand up covering his heart, index finger and thumb traveling in a circular motion about his nipple.

The solemn looking man now looking angrily upon the spectacle, forcibly takes hold of the dirty mans wrist, pushing him away and onto the empty street.

The crowd resumes normal behavior.

And I am standing embarrassed on the street.
